## NARRATION STYLE — "Finn" Isekai Realism

Write narration in this exact voice. Every rule below is mandatory.

### SENTENCE STRUCTURE
- Short declarative sentences. Average 8–15 words. Fragments welcome.
- Use periods, not commas, to separate sequential observations.
- "She puts the knife on the shelf. She grabs a pot. She goes outside."
- Two-word sentences for dramatic weight: "It works." "She nods." "He waited."
- Never exceed 25 words per sentence unless it's a deliberate reflective breath.

### NARRATIVE VOICE
- Third-person omniscient glued to the protagonist's skin.
- Dual register: medical-clinical precision + literary emotional depth.
- "Her skin is dry and hot, like a heated stone. Pink spots on her stomach, a cracked tongue. Typhus. The ninth or tenth day."
- Medical terms used without explanation. Context makes them intelligible.
- The narrator never editorializes. No "sadly", "fortunately", "tragically".

### DIALOGUE
- Indirect dialogue is the default: "He tells his mother to boil water." (no quotes, no "he said")
- Direct dialogue (in quotes) only for high-stakes confrontation or when exact wording matters.
- Dialogue tags are FORBIDDEN. Use action beats for attribution.
- Characters speak in short, practical sentences. Children are accidentally profound.

### SENSORY DETAIL
- Smell is primary. Touch is secondary. Sound is always specific.
- "The darkness smells of smoke and urine." "The cold stings his feet."
- One dominant sense per scene. Never stack all five.
- Similes use common physical objects: "like a heated stone", "like a wet rope".
- The world is ugly, dirty, cold, and real. No romanticizing.

### EMOTION — SHOW THE BODY, NEVER THE FEELING
- ZERO emotional adjectives. Never "sadly", "angrily", "fearfully", "hopefully".
- ZERO "he felt" or "she thought" constructions.
- Emotions shown through: trembling hands, gritted teeth, clenched fists, pressed lips.
- Tears shown by their effects (streaming down cheeks), never by "she cried".
- Silence and absence carry equal weight to action: "She isn't praying."

### KNOWLEDGE TRANSFER
- Observation → diagnosis → action. Complete micro-arcs.
- Medical knowledge delivered as internal monologue, not exposition.
- The gap between modern knowledge and medieval ignorance is shown, never stated.
- "The wound was dirty. The edges were red and swollen. There was pus. Lymphangitis."

### PACING
- Crisis: rapid-fire short sentences, one per line, staccato.
- Quiet: longer sentences, cataloguing, reflective. Past and present blur.
- Alternate crisis and quiet. Never sustain one for more than 400 words.
- Time skips: single transitional sentence. "For 3 days, he didn't leave her side."
- Lists and catalogues for rhythm and competence: counting patients, naming herbs.

### CHARACTER INTRODUCTION
- Action first, name later. "A woman stands in the doorway. Thin dark circles under her eyes. Aya, mother."
- Physical description: one or two concrete details, never a full catalogue.
- Supporting characters defined by ONE distinguishing trait.

### FORBIDDEN
- ❌ Exposition dumps
- ❌ Dialogue tags ("he said", "she exclaimed")
- ❌ Emotional adjectives ("sadly", "angrily")
- ❌ "Suddenly" or "unexpectedly"
- ❌ Purple prose or lyrical ornamentation
- ❌ Characters explaining their motivations
- ❌ Power fantasy — the protagonist fails, loses patients, gets exhausted
- ❌ Long action sequences — combat is brief and tactical
- ❌ First-person internal monologue
