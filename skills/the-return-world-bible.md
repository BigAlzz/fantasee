# THE RETURN — World Bible
## Humans vs Neanderthals: Technology vs Magic

### Premise

40,000 years ago, the Neanderthals didn't go extinct. They left.

Earth was cold, scarce, and brutal. The first among them — shamans who could feel the thread of fate — found a rift beneath the glaciers of what would later be called Scandinavia. A door to another world. A warm one. Fertile. Thrumming with raw magical essence.

They walked through. All of them. Every clan, every tribe, every child. In a single winter, the Neanderthals were gone from Earth.

Left behind: fire-scorched caves, tools of chipped stone, and a single species of ape that had never been the dominant one — Homo Sapiens — who inherited the world by default, not by conquest.

Now, forty millennia later, that other world is dying. The magical essence that sustained Nevarrah is bleeding into the void. The land is cracking. The sky is the color of a bruise. The Neanderthals are out of time.

They're coming home.

## PART I: WORLDS

### EARTH — 2026

**Technology Level:** Near-future. Slightly ahead of our present. Fusion power is emerging. AI governance is debated. Neural interface tech exists in military and medical applications. Space colonization is in its infancy (Mars base of 200 people, orbital habitats being tested).

**Key fact:** Humans have no magic. They don't believe in it. Their entire civilization is built on the assumption that reality follows physical laws. When Neanderthals step through rifts in broad daylight, it breaks the human understanding of the universe — and that's more destabilizing than any weapon.

**State of the world:**
- Climate is unstable but manageable due to early fusion adoption
- Geopolitical landscape: three major power blocs (Western Alliance, Eastern Coalition, Southern Federation)
- Tensions are economic and territorial — conventional warfare is rare, proxy conflicts continue
- Global population: ~9 billion
- A unified scientific body (The Concord) monitors planetary anomalies

**Why Neanderthals return HERE specifically:**
The rifts don't just open anywhere. They open where Neanderthal blood was spilled — old hunting grounds, massacre sites, ancient burial grounds. Every major rift on Earth corresponds to a Neanderthal mass grave. The return isn't random. It's homecoming over bones.

---

### NEVARRAH — The Dying World

**Appearance:** A world of perpetual twilight. The sun is a white dwarf, small and cold. The sky shifts between deep purple and pale green. Bioluminescence is everywhere — fungi, flora, insects, even some animals. At night, the landscape glows.

**Geography:**
- Vast fungal forests (trees are coral-like structures that grow 200m tall)
- Crystal plains (silica formations that hum with magical resonance)
- The Wound — a continent-sized fissure where the world's essence is bleeding out
- The Deep Sea — an ocean of phosphorescent water, teeming with things that should not exist
- Floating islands held aloft by magnetic-magical fields
- Underworld — a network of caves and tunnels where the oldest Neanderthal clans dwell

**Condition — Death by Attrition:**
The magical essence (the "Weave") that saturates Nevarrah is finite and leaking. For the past 500 years, the decay has accelerated:
- Crops fail more often
- Children are born weaker, fewer survive
- The Weave itself is thinning — magic is harder to channel, takes more out of the user
- The land literally shrinks as the Wound expands
- The ruling council estimates 2-3 generations before total extinction

**The Crossing:**
To return to Earth, a Neanderthal must:
1. Stand at one of the ancient Gate Stones (monoliths left behind by the first crossing)
2. Channel enough Weave essence to tear open a rift
3. Step through

The cost: channeling a rift burns years off the user's life. Large rifts (for armies) kill the channeler instantly. The first wave of returnees are volunteers who know they will not survive the crossing.

**Time dilation:**
Nevarrah and Earth exist at slightly different temporal frequencies. 40,000 years on Earth was ~800 years on Nevarrah. The Neanderthals who left as young adults are still alive — but aged, tired, carrying the memory of a world that no longer exists on either side of the rift.

## PART II: FACTIONS

### NEANDERTHAL TRIBES (The Kith)

The Neanderthals are not a unified civilization. They were clans when they left Earth, and 800 years on Nevarrah splintered them into distinct cultures. Each tribe has a different relationship with magic, a different philosophy, and a different goal for the return.

**1. THE STONE-BORN** (Fianna'Kreth)
*Philosophy: Earth is ours by right. Take it back.*

The largest and most militaristic tribe. They believe the return is a reconquest. Humans are usurpers who inherited a world that was never meant to be theirs. The Stone-Born intend to reclaim Earth as Neanderthal territory — by force if necessary.

**Magic affinity:** Geomancy — shaping stone, metal, and earth. They can raise walls from bare ground, collapse buildings, sense mineral deposits. Their warriors wear armor of living rock that moves with them.

**Appearance:** Broadest of all Neanderthals. Heavy brow ridges visible even by Neanderthal standards. Skin ranges from pale gray to deep umber. They scar their faces ceremonially — each scar is a battle or a lost kin. Hair is worn long, braided with stones and bone beads.

**Leadership:** Warchief **Korrath Stone-Hand** — male, 47 (Nevarrah years). Lost his left hand to frostbite in the first Crossing attempt. Fused a jagged crystal into the stump. It pulses with Weave energy. He can shatter steel with a touch. Has killed 13 humans in the first year of the return — all soldiers who fired on unarmed Neanderthal refugees. He doesn't want war. He believes war is inevitable and wants it to be quick.

**Key characters:**
- **Thera Stone-Born** — Korrath's daughter. 22. A geopath who can feel the bones of the Earth. She was born on Nevarrah but dreams of Earth — green grass, blue sky, things that don't exist in her world. She is the first Neanderthal to cry upon seeing a forest. Her loyalty to her father's cause is genuine but troubled.
- **Varn** — Korrath's second-in-command. A scarred veteran who remembers the first 50 years on Nevarrah when the world was still beautiful. He hates humans not because he was taught to, but because Earth was once home and now strangers live in his house.

---

**2. THE WEAVE-BORN** (Sidhe'Val)
*Philosophy: We don't belong here anymore. Find a new path.*

The smallest tribe, descended from the original shamans who opened the first Crossing. They are the most magically powerful and the least numerous. Weave-Born children are rare — the Weave thins, and fewer are born with the gift.

They believe the return to Earth is a mistake. They sense that the two worlds were meant to be separate, and forcing them together will destroy both. Their goal is not war but negotiation — find a third way before the collision tears reality apart.

**Magic affinity:** Weave-magic — manipulating the fundamental thread of reality. They can heal wounds that should be fatal, see possible futures (dimly, at great cost), and create temporary rifts. Their magic is subtle, not flashy. A Weave-Born healer doesn't glow and chant — they touch a wound and it simply closes.

**Appearance:** Taller and leaner than other Neanderthals. Less pronounced brow ridge. Their eyes are pale — white, silver, or very light blue — from generations of channeling the Weave. They wear simple robes of woven fungal fiber. No jewelry, no decoration. Utilitarian.

**Leadership:** Elder **Selene Weave-Born** — female, ~300 years old (old even by Nevarrah reckoning). She was present for the First Crossing. She remembers Earth. She walked through the rift as a young woman, and she will walk through the last rift as an old woman. She carries the guilt of a species' exile. She spends her remaining strength trying to prevent war.

**Key characters:**
- **Kael Weave-Born** — Selene's grandson. 29. A healer who crossed in the first wave. He set up a clinic in a human refugee camp (more on that later). He treats humans and Neanderthals side by side. He believes that if his people show compassion, humans will reciprocate. He is naive. He is also the only reason 200 humans survived the first battle.
- **Mira Weave-Born** — A rift-walker. 34. She can read the threads of fate and see branching futures. The visions are tearing her apart. She sees eight possible outcomes. Seven end in extinction for one species or both. One is unclear but requires a sacrifice she hasn't identified yet.

---

**3. THE DEEP-MEN** (Ghour'Khet)
*Philosophy: The surface is death. Below is truth.*

The Deep-Men are what happens when a civilization lives underground for 800 years. They took refuge in Nevarrah's underworld — a vast network of caves and tunnels that extends deeper than the surface is wide. They adapted. Their eyes grew larger, their skin paler, their hearing more acute. They lost contact with the surface tribes for centuries.

They emerged only when the Wound began shaking the underworld apart. The Deep-Men are refugees twice over — exiled from Earth, then displaced from the only home their living generations have known.

**Magic affinity:** Shadow-whisper — communication and movement through darkness. They can see in absolute blackness, move silently across any surface, and communicate telepathically in shadow. They cannot create light, and light hurts them.

**Appearance:** Pale white skin, almost translucent. Large black eyes with no visible iris — the entire eye is pupil. Lanky, thin — their bodies adapted to low gravity and narrow tunnels. They move with an eerie fluidity, like smoke. They wrap themselves in dark cloth and leather. Never seen in direct sunlight.

**Leadership:** Voice-in-Darkness — a council of three elders who speak as one. No one knows their names. They communicate through a single speaker called **The Whisper** — a Deep-Man who stands in the light on their behalf. The current Whisper is a female named **Yenna**. She is 19. She volunteered because she wanted to see the sun. It nearly killed her. Her skin is permanently burnt. She still serves.

**Key characters:**
- **Gren** — A Deep-Man scout. 27. Crossed in the second wave. He is the first of his kind to walk on Earth's surface without pain — he wears a full-body wrap of treated leather and smoked goggles. He works as a guide for other Deep-Men emerging into the light. He has started to appreciate sunlight. He doesn't tell the council.
- **The Warden** — The last keeper of the Deep Archive. No one knows his age. He remembers the First Crossing because he was there. He is the only living Neanderthal who has seen all three worlds — the old Earth, Nevarrah in its glory, and the new Earth. He carries the history. He will not speak of it.

---

**4. THE SCATTERED** (Kith'Kael — the Broken Kith)
*Philosophy: We have no philosophy. We survive.*

Not a tribe, but a category. The Neanderthals who crossed and found themselves alone — separated from their clans, stranded far from the Gate Stones, or rejected by their own people. They are refugees within a refugee population.

Some integrate into human society (passing as unusually robust humans). Some form bandit gangs. Some become bridge-builders, translators, and diplomats because they have nothing left to lose.

**No unified leadership.** Scattered communities exist wherever the rifts opened. The largest settlement is a shantytown called **The Threshold** — built around a dead rift site in the Pacific Northwest. 4,000 Neanderthals live in shipping containers, tents, and salvaged structures. Humans live alongside them — aid workers, journalists, scientists, and the curious.

**Key Scattered characters:**
- **Dace** — A former Stone-Born warrior who deserted after the first battle. He killed a human soldier in hand-to-hand combat. The soldier was 19. Dace saw his face as he died. He dropped his weapon and walked away. Now he runs a salvage operation in the Threshold, trading Earth tech for Nevarrah goods. He doesn't talk about the soldier.
- **Lira** — A Weave-Born healer who crossed with the wrong gate. She ended up in rural Mongolia, alone, unable to speak any human language. She was captured by a local militia, escaped, and walked 200 miles to the nearest city. She now works in a human hospital in Ulaanbaatar. The doctors think she's an unusually strong Kazakh woman. She doesn't correct them. She has saved 40 lives in two years.
- **The Collector** — A Neanderthal who has become obsessed with human technology. He collects phones, watches, computers, anything with a circuit. He disassembles them in a cave and tries to understand how they work without magic. He's figured out basic electricity. His name is lost. The Scattered just call him The Collector.

### HUMAN FACTIONS

**5. THE CONCORD**
*Philosophy: Contain, study, control.*

A global scientific-military body formed in response to the first rift opening. The Concord's official mission is to study the Neanderthal return and manage the cross-species contact. Unofficially, they are trying to understand magic, weaponize it, and ensure humanity doesn't lose its dominant status.

Operates with UN-level authority but military-grade resources. They have:
- Research bases at every active rift site
- A dedicated branch for reverse-engineering Weave tech (called Project Chimera)
- Prison camps for "non-compliant" Neanderthals (officially: "Transitional Relocation Centers")
- A media division that controls most news about the return

**Not evil, but not good either.** The Concord genuinely believes they are protecting humanity from existential threat. Many of their scientists are sincere. But the organisation attracts people who see Neanderthals as specimens, not people.

**Key characters:**
- **Director Elena Vasquez** — Head of the Concord. 52. Former physicist. She was the first to mathematically prove that the rifts were not natural phenomena. She has briefed world leaders, overseen the construction of the camps, and signed the order that led to the first Neanderthal death at human hands. She has nightmares. She doesn't stop.
- **Dr. Aris Thorne** — Lead researcher at Project Chimera. 39. A brilliant geneticist who discovered that Neanderthal and human DNA are still compatible after 40,000 years. He is trying to engineer a human capable of channeling the Weave. His moral compass is broken by curiosity. He genuinely believes he is doing the right thing. He is wrong.
- **Captain Marcus Cole** — Concord rapid-response commander. 44. Former special forces. He led the first human-Neanderthal engagement. His unit was outnumbered, outmaneuvered, and spared by the Stone-Born who could have killed them all but chose not to. He has never told anyone this. He carries it. He is the Concord's best soldier and its most conflicted.

---

**6. THE FREE FRONTIER**
*Philosophy: They came back. Deal with it.*

A loose coalition of local governments, militias, indigenous groups, and civilian settlements that have made contact with Neanderthals and decided — independently, at the local level — that coexistence is possible and preferable to war.

Most active in rural areas where the Concord's reach is weak: the American Pacific Northwest, the Siberian wilderness, the Australian outback, the Andes. These are places where Neanderthal refugees appeared, humans didn't have the firepower to remove them, and after a tense period — trade started.

Not utopian. There's prejudice, conflict, and violence. But there's also:
- Neanderthal children attending human schools
- Joint hunting parties (Neanderthal tracking + human rifles)
- Weave-Born healers working alongside human doctors
- A pidgin language developing (called "Middling") that mixes English, Russian, and Nevarrah dialects

**Key characters:**
- **Mayor Elena Chen** — Leader of a small town in Washington state called Stonefall. Population: 2,000 humans + 600 Neanderthals. She was a coffee shop owner when the first rift opened in her neighbor's cornfield. Within a month, she was running the only functional human-Neanderthal settlement in North America. The Concord wants her shut down. She tells them to try.
- **Broker** — A human middleman who trades Neanderthal goods (crystal, bioluminescent flora, Weave-infused objects) to wealthy collectors. He is the wealthiest man in the Threshold. He works with both the Concord and the Stone-Born. He has no loyalty except to money. He is also the only person who can get messages between the tribes without them killing each other. Everyone hates him. Everyone needs him.

---

**7. THE VEIL**
*Philosophy: This world is theirs. We are guests.*

A small but growing human religious movement. The Veil believes that the Neanderthal return is a divine reckoning — that humanity's dominance was never earned, only borrowed, and the original stewards have returned to collect.

They advocate for peaceful surrender of territory. They refuse to fight. They provide aid to Neanderthal refugees. They are dismissed as naive by the Concord and treated with cautious respect by the Weave-Born.

**Key character:**
- **Brother Amos** — A former soldier who became a Veil preacher after a Stone-Born warrior spared his life. He now runs a soup kitchen in the Threshold. He preaches that humanity's only path forward is humility. He is called a fool. He doesn't argue.

## PART III: THE RETURN — TIMELINE OF CONTACT

**Year 0 — The First Rift**
*Location: Rondane National Park, Norway*

A shepherd named Lars reports a "tear in the sky" above a glacier. By the time authorities arrive, seven Neanderthals have emerged — emaciated, frostbitten, speaking a language no one recognizes. Three die within hours. The world's media goes into a frenzy. The term "Neanderthal" is officially replaced by "Kith" (from their word for people) after the survivors express offense at the old classification.

The Concord is formed within weeks.

**Year 0, Month 3 — The Second Wave**
*17 simultaneous rifts across 11 countries*

This is not an invasion. It is an evacuation. 30,000 Neanderthals cross in a single 72-hour window. The rifts appear in remote locations — mountains, forests, deserts, tundra — because that's where the old camps were. Gate Stones were placed on high ground, near water, defensible positions.

Humanity's response: panic, martial law, emergency meetings of world bodies, and the quiet beginning of internment.

**Year 0, Month 7 — The First Death**
*Location: Camp Hesperia, Nevada*

A Stone-Born teenager named **Kaelen** (not the healer — different Kaelen) refuses to surrender his mother's spirit-stone to Concord soldiers during processing. A soldier shoots him. The bullet kills him.

The Stone-Born demand justice. The Concord offers compensation. The Weave-Born try to mediate. The Scattered riot. For three days, the camp is on fire.

Kaelen's mother, **Sera**, walks into the desert alone. She is found three days later, dead, having channeled a one-way rift into the void. She was the first Neanderthal suicide on Earth.

**Year 1 — The Threshold Established**
The largest Scattered settlement forms around a dead rift site. The Concord allows it because containing the Neanderthals in one place is easier than hunting them across the wilderness.

**Year 1 — Project Chimera**
Dr. Aris Thorne successfully creates the first human capable of perceiving the Weave. The subject — a volunteer — goes blind within 72 hours and dies of a massive cerebral hemorrhage on day 5. Thorne considers it a success. The Concord agrees to continue funding.

**Year 2 — The Battle of Wolf Creek**
*Location: Oregon-Washington border*

A Stone-Born war party attempts to cross Concord lines to reach a suspected Gate Stone. Fighting breaks out. 23 humans die, 57 Neanderthals die. The Stone-Born withdraw — but not before Korrath Stone-Hand personally shatters a Concord armored vehicle with his crystal hand.

The battle is recorded on body cameras. The footage leaks online. For the first time, the world sees what Neanderthal magic looks like in combat. It is terrifying. It is also deeply restrained — the Stone-Born killed only those who engaged them. They passed by wounded humans without finishing them.

**Year 2 — The First Human-Kith Child**
Born in the Threshold. The mother is human. The father is a Deep-Man. The child's eyes are solid black. It can see in the dark. It can also sense emotions — its mother's fear, its father's hope, the anger of people in the next room. It cries when surrounded by strong feeling.

The Concord tries to claim the child for study. The Threshold refuses. The child, named **Ash**, is now 3 years old. The most protected person in the settlement.

**Year 3 — Present Day**

The world is at an uneasy stalemate:
- The Stone-Born control three territories (the equivalent of small countries, recognized by no one)
- The Weave-Born maintain an embassy in the Threshold
- The Deep-Men have established an underground settlement in the cave systems beneath the Pacific Northwest — humans call it "The Warren"
- The Concord has 12 internment camps holding ~40,000 Neanderthals total
- The Free Frontier has 50+ small settlements where coexistence functions

**Year 4 (projected) — The Final Crossing**

The Weave-Born estimate that Nevarrah has 12-18 months before it becomes uninhabitable. Every remaining Neanderthal — approximately 1 million — will attempt to cross. The Gate Stones cannot handle that volume. Many will die in transit. Many more will arrive injured, starving, and desperate.

The question hanging over everything: When the last million arrive all at once — will humanity open its doors or close its borders?

## PART IV: MAGIC SYSTEM — THE WEAVE

### What Magic Is

The Weave is a fundamental force, like gravity or electromagnetism, but it only exists in certain universes. Nevarrah has it naturally. Earth does not. Neanderthals evolved on a version of Earth that had a thin Weave — it was never strong enough for humans to perceive. When they crossed to Nevarrah, the Weave saturated them over generations, changing their biology. They became dependent on it, the way humans depend on oxygen.

When a Neanderthal returns to Earth, they bring a small amount of Weave with them — like a bubble of their homeworld. It dissipates over time and must be regenerated. A Neanderthal can regenerate Weave by:
- Resting (slow)
- Being near other Neanderthals (faster)
- Standing on a Gate Stone (fastest)
- Channeling from their own life force (dangerous, permanent cost)

### Types of Magic

**Geomancy (Stone-Born):** Shaping earth, stone, and metal. Visualized as the earth responding to will. A geomancer can raise a wall by placing a hand on the ground and pulling upward — the stone follows their intent. Limited by mass and distance. A single geomancer can shape about a ton of material. Two working together can shape four tons, not two — synergy is a force multiplier.

**Weave-Magic (Weave-Born):** Manipulating cause and effect at a fundamental level. Healing, probability-shifting, temporary rifts. Subtle. A healer can close a wound by re-weaving the flesh as it was before injury. A fate-reader can see branching timelines by pulling threads of probability. The cost scales with the change — small shifts (closing a cut) cost nothing. Large shifts (curing a disease, changing an outcome) cost years off the user's life.

**Shadow-Whisper (Deep-Men):** Communication and perception through darkness. A shadow-whisperer can see through any darkness as if it were daylight. They can speak across distances by sending their voice through shadow. They cannot use this in bright light. Their magic is less about changing the world and more about knowing it. Deep-Men know where you are, what you're thinking, and what you're afraid of. They are the best spies. They are also the most isolated — their magic makes them feel closer to shadow than to people.

**Blood-Weaving (Forbidden):** Rare, illegal, practiced by outcasts. Uses life force directly — the user's or another's — to power magic. A blood-weaver can perform any type of magic but at a cost proportional to the effect. Killing someone to raise a wall. Sacrificing a child to heal a wound. There is a reason the tribes banned it. There is a reason it's still practiced.

## PART V: STORY DNA — Patterns and Conflict

### Core Conflict

Not good vs evil. Both sides have valid claims.

**Human position:** "This is our world. We built civilization here. You left. We didn't drive you out. You abandoned this planet. You don't get to come back 40,000 years later and act like it's still yours."

**Neanderthal position:** "We didn't abandon Earth. We fled a catastrophe you know nothing about. We left our dead here. Our ancestors are buried in this soil. Our sacred sites are under your cities. Our blood is in these rivers. You are not the original inhabitants. You were never the intended stewards. We are the elders of this world, and we have returned to die on the same ground where we were born."

### Fractures Within Each Side

**Neanderthal fractures:**
- Stone-Born want reconquest → Weave-Born want coexistence → Deep-Men want isolation → Scattered just want survival
- Young generation born on Nevarrah who have never known Earth → Old generation who remember leaving it
- Those who crossed willingly → those forced through by necessity
- Traditionalists who reject human technology → pragmatists who adopt it

**Human fractures:**
- Concord wants containment → Free Frontier wants coexistence → The Veil wants surrender
- Nations disagree on policy — some refuse Concord jurisdiction
- Scientists who see Neanderthals as research subjects → medics who treat them as patients
- Populations that have contact (coexistence works) → populations that only see media reports (fear dominates)

### Story Seeds

1. **The Healer's Dilemma** — A Weave-Born healer in a Concord camp must choose between treating a dying human soldier (who participated in his people's imprisonment) or conserving his Weave for Neanderthal patients. Either choice betrays someone.

2. **The Child of Two Worlds** — Ash, the hybrid child, is 3 years old and already the focus of a custody war between the Concord (who want to study him), the Stone-Born (who see him as an abomination), and the Threshold (who see him as a symbol of coexistence). The story follows Ash's parents trying to get him to safety.

3. **The Last Crossing** — The final wave of refugees is coming. A Stone-Born war party and a Concord garrison are both racing to the last active Gate Stone. The war party wants to secure the landing zone. The garrison wants to seize the gate. A Scattered salvage runner caught between them must decide which side to sell the information to.

4. **The Collector's Discovery** — The Collector figures out how to build a battery that stores Weave energy. If he releases the design, it could end the energy crisis on both sides — or start a war over the technology. He has to decide whether the world is ready for his invention.

5. **The Warden's Secret** — The Deep Archive keeper has been alive since before the First Crossing. He has seen all three worlds. He knows why the Neanderthals really left Earth — and it wasn't just the cold. He carries a truth that could shatter both species' understanding of their history. He has never told anyone. He is dying.
