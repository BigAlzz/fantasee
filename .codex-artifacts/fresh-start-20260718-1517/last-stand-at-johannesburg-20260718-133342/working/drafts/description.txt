When the sky split open above Johannesburg and the invaders descended in fire and bone-white metal, it was the battered soldiers of the 12th Hillside Regiment who held the ridge — untrained for cosmic horrors, armed with nothing but sand-worn rifles and the stubborn fury of men who refused to die kneeling. Beneath a bruised violet sky, the veldt ran slick with blood both human and *other*, and still the line held, each fallen soldier dragged aside so another could fill the gap in the crumbling barricade of earth and prayer. By nightfall, the dead outnumbered the living ten to one, yet not a single soul abandoned that hill — because behind them lay Johannesburg, and behind Johannesburg lay everything that still meant *home*.
