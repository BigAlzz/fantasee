The Hillside Stand began not with a global alarm, but with the screaming of metal and the sudden, brutal purple of an alien sky over the Johannesburg veld. The South African Army unit dug in on the ridge soon learned their new enemy did not just invade—they harvested, and the rich soil of the earth was to be their threshing floor. This was not a battle for victory, but a grim, frantic line drawn in the burning dust, a last defiant stand for a world that had already begun to end.
