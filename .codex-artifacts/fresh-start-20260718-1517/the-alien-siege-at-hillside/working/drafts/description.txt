When the sky above Johannesburg split open in a wound of black fire, the men of Hillside learned that hell had teeth and they were already upon them. Outgunned, outmanned, and abandoned to the dust, the South African soldiers held their crumbling ridge as unearthly horrors clawed across the veldt, turning the golden Highveld sunset into a slaughterhouse glow. By dawn, there would be no victory—only survivors who wished there weren't.
