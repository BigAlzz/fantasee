The veld trembled under the hellish glow of descending ships as Sergeant Ndaba and his shorthanded unit dug in on Hillside, the last solid ground before Johannesburg's suburbs. What emerged from the impact craters were no mere invaders, but crystalline monstrosities—a tide of geometric rage that turned their bullets to glittering dust. This was not a battle for victory, but a brutal, desperate lesson in human frailty, written in blood on the ridge as the alien dawn broke.
