When the sky split open above Johannesburg and the alien horde came screaming down like a biblical plague, the battered soldiers of the 12th Infantry were the only thing standing between the invaders and ten million souls. Armed with little more than sandbagged positions and dogged resolve, they dug into the hillside and sold every inch of ground in blood and bone. By dawn, the ridge was a graveyard of both men and monsters—but the city behind them still burned with a desperate, defiant light.
