When the sky split open above Johannesburg and spewed forth a tide of chitinous nightmares, it was the battered soldiers of the Hillside Stand—dug into the sun-scorched ridgeline like desperate teeth in a broken jaw—who met them first. Outgunned, outmanned, and drenched in the blood of their fallen, the surviving unit fights a losing war through smoke-choked streets and rubble, where every shadow births something monstrous and every bullet is a prayer to a god that isn't listening. The Hillside Stand will not hold—but how many of the alien horrors can they drag into the grave with them?
