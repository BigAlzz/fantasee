When the sky split open over Johannesburg, raining fire and malformed flesh upon the veld, it was the bloodied and under-equipped soldiers of Hillside Stand who answered first. Armed with nothing but rust-eaten rifles and a stubbornness bred into their bones, they held the ridgeline against horrors that spoke in frequencies that shattered the mind. By dawn, the hill was theirs — a scarred altar of spent brass and black ichor — but something vast and patient still stirred beyond the smoke, and the ground beneath them had begun to remember what it once was before mankind ever walked upon it.
