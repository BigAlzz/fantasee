The air over the Johannesburg ridge smelled of ozone and panic as the Hillside Stand—the last unit on the line—watched the sky tear open. What descended wasn't metal, but chitinous, chittering horror that moved with a speed that made the disciplined crack of their rifles sound like a child’s futile protest. This was not a battle; it was a baptism by fire and acid, the first brutal note in a war humanity was not meant to survive.
